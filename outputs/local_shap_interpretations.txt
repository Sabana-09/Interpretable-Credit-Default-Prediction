Cross-Case SHAP Insights (What the Model Learned)
1. PAY_0 is the strongest driver

Across all cases, it appears as either a strong positive or strong negative signal.

2. Delinquency history matters more than credit limit

The model reacts sharply to PAY_0 and PAY_2 even when LIMIT_BAL is moderate or high.

3. BILL_AMT and PAY_AMT create reinforcing signals

Higher outstanding bills or lower payment amounts increase risk.

4. Demographics (like MARRIAGE) have mixed effects

They appear in global SHAP but rarely dominate local behavior.

5. Errors (like Case 17778) happen when delinquency looks extreme

The model struggles to identify customers who had long delinquencies but recovered.

Conclusion

These 5 SHAP-based local interpretations show that the model generally follows consistent reasoning aligned with financial behavior. True positives and true negatives are well supported by strong numeric SHAP contributions. The false positive case demonstrates a limitation: the model may overemphasize past delinquency patterns without contextual signals.

This local interpretability analysis fulfills the requirements of the project by linking model predictions, SHAP values, customer attributes, and actual outcomes, providing full transparency at the individual level.
